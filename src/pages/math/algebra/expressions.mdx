An **algebraic expression** is a combination of **numbers**, **variables**, **operators**, and **constants**, that represents a value.

- **Term**: A part of an expression.
- **Variable**: A letter that stands for a number.
- **Constant**: A number that stays the same.
- **Coefficient**: A number that multiplies a variable.
- **Operator**: A symbol for a math operation (like +, -, ร, รท).

## Simplifying and Combining Like Terms

<Image src={Image2} alt='Combining like terms example'/>

You can simplify and combine like terms by grouping together the ones that are alike.


### Examples
 
> $
3x + 5x - 2x\\
8x - 2x\\
6x
$
  
> $
7x + 4y - 2x + 5y - 3\\
5x + 4y + 5y - 3\\
5x + 9y - 3
$

> $
x(2x + 3) - 3(4 - x)\\
2x^2 + 3x - 12 + 3x\\ 
2x^2 + 6x - 12
$

> $
2x(x + 3) + 3x^2 - 8x + 1\\
2x^2 + 6x + 3x^2 - 8x + 1\\
5x^2 -2x + 1
$
