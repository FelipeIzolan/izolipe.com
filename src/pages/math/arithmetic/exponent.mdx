The **exponent** of a number indicates **how many times** the **number should multiply itself**.

### Example:

**5** to the power of **3** is interpreted as:
- $5^3$
- $5 * 5 * 5$
- $125$

---

## Negative Exponent

The **negative exponent** means the reciprocal of $x$ raised to the power of $n$.

> $
x^{-n} = \frac{1}{x^n}\\
8^{-3} = \frac{1}{8^3}
$

---

## Exponent 0 & 1

The **exponent 1** means the **number itself**.\
Any number without an explicit exponent has an exponent of 1.

> $
x^1 = x\\
5^1 = 5
$


The **exponent 0** means the **number 1**.

> $
x^0 = 1\\
5^0 = 1
$

---

## Exponent Rules

- **Product Rule**: <br/> $x^m \times x^n = x^{m + n}$
- **Quotinent Rule**: <br/> $x^m / x^n = x^{m - n}$
- **Power of Power Rule**: <br/> $(x^m)^n = x^{m \times n}$
- **Power of a Product Rule**: <br/> $(xy)^n = x^n y^n$
- **Power of a Quotinent Rule**: <br/> $(\frac{x}{y})^n = \frac{x^n}{y^n}$
- **Fractional Exponent Rule**: <br/> $x^{\frac{m}{n}} = \sqrt[n]{x^m}$
