The **logarithm** determines **how many times** a **number (the base)** must be **raised to a power** to **produce another number (the argument)**.

---

## Logarithms & Exponents

<Image src={Image2} alt="Logarithm and Exponents relationship" />

> $
\log_bx=y\\
b^y=x
$

---

## Negative Logarithm

The negative logarithm indicates that **the number is less than 1.**
> $-\log_bx = \log_b\frac{1}{b}$

---

## Logarithm 0 & 1

The **logarithm of 1** is always **0**
> $\log_b1 = 0$\
> $b^0 = 1$


The **logarithm** with a **base equal to the argument** is always **1**.
> $\log_bb = 1$\
> $b^1 = b$

---

## Natural Logarithm

<Image src={Image3} alt="Natural Logarithm" />
The **natural logarithm** is a logarithm with **base $e$ (Euler's number)**.

---

## Logarithm Rules

- **Product Rule**: <br/> $\log_nxy = \log_nx + \log_ny$
- **Quotient Rule**: <br/> $\log_n\frac{x}{y} = \log_nx - \log_ny$
- **Power Rule**: <br/> $\log_nx^y = y \times log_nx$
- **Negative Rule**: <br/> $-\log_nx = \log_n\frac{1}{x}$
- **Change of Base Rule**: <br/> $\log_nx = \frac{\log x}{\log n}$
