Count nouns are things you can count as individual items and have singular and plural forms.

---

## Singular & Plural

Countable nouns have two forms: singular (for one thing) and plural (for more than one thing).

---

### Singular

<Image src={Image2} alt='an apple'/>

A singular noun refers to one person, place, thing, or idea. It usually needs a **determiner (a, the, my, ...)**.

#### Examples:

- Felipe ate **an apple**.
- There's **a beach** near here.
- **My dog** is sleeping.
- I only have **one dollar**.
- **The book** is on the table.

---

### Plural

<Image src={Image3} alt='apples'/>

A plural noun refers to two or more person, place, thing or idea.

#### Examples:

- Felipe ate some **apples**.
- There are **beaches** near here.
- **My dogs** are sleeping.
- I only have **two dollars**.
- **The books** are on the table.

#### Singular to Plural

Usually, **-s** is added to make a noun plural.

- Banana → Bananas;
- Bottle → Bottles;
- Door → Doors;

For nouns ending in **-s**, **-ss**, **-ch**, **-sh**, **-x**, or **-o**, add **-es** to make them plural.

- Bus → Buses;
- Kiss → Kisses;
- Sandwich → Sandwiches;
- Dish → Dishes;
- Tax → Taxes;
- Hero → Heroes;

...
